Projectile
Motion

If you didn't read the last lesson on gravity and aren't familiar with basic physics terms such as position, velocity, and acceleration I would highly recommend you go back and read the previous lesson as it clears these terms up. Projectile motion is one of the most used physics concepts in video games, most notably in first person shooters. Projectile motion refers to how an object moves across the screen when it is affected by some initial force, and the force of gravity. In shooters, this is used to simulate bullet drop. Without projectile motion in your game, bullets would travel an infinite distance. So, what is projectile motion in our use case? Usually, we will have gravity acting on the object as well as the object having an initial velocity in the x direction with everything else set to 0. So, in each frame, the acceleration of gravity is added to the velocity in the y direction (keep in mind this is a positive number due to the range of pixels), which is added to the objects y position; and for the x direction, the initial x velocity is added to the x position. The combinations of these two will move your object in a smooth, curved path until it hits the ground (or plunges to infinity if you don't have your object stop when it hits the ground). In case you are interested the general equation for projectile motion is: xf=xi+vit+at2 where x can be subbed for y, xi is the initial x position, vi is the initial velocity, t is time which is in frames for our use.
